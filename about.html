Modern Lives and Spirituality

We do what we do. Our work. We have what we have. Our families. And that’s the central most important aspect for us. We seek to fulfil our ambition, responsibility, our possessions and comfort and raise the standard or quality of life. At the same time, if we carefully notice, We strive to work with sincerity and passion towards where we can help others , try and be wise about life and our interactions and actions and also seek to be generally nice people. To do all of this as well. we must do effort - To sort of be selfless or unselfish. That effort to be sincere, wise, helpful and nice is spiritual practice in very simple terms. This can and in fact should be knitted in our daily lives and not necessarily by renouncing. That’s the pursuit or problem for all the modern roles of all of ours, be it professionals homemakers parents students entrepreneurs or whatever role we may be having in social setup.

Spiritual practice may or may not have anything to do with religion. (Because one needn’t have any dogma attached to it or a worship attached to it or a belief system attached to it.) Spiritual practice is our life reflecting and expressing our inherent human qualities. Like generosity patience energy caring wisdom et al. Emphasising awareness and practising awareness is the basis of spiritual practice. In a way state of pure ‘be-ing’ what we are. Again thereby nothing religious about it. Spiritual practice is thereby about building and sustaining that balance in our day to day lives. Its about joy of our being.

Last 100 years has had terrific growth of tech as well as abundance of material goods. Good and bad. Clearly good from a point of view of the basic strife for necessities is getting over for many a people in society. Leaves much more time in their heads. Which if constantly filled with more and more ambition leaves them stressed. How do we balance becomes central question then. And increasingly more people are coming to this cross roads. There is imbalance. The imbalance in real terms is merely the extreme external focus (towards what we do plus material goods toward what we can possess) versus the focus on who we really are, what we really are. Spiritual practice in our day to day life, the very same life and environment that we have, IS and creates that balance. Simmr aims to achieve this for all of us who are here.
